Ludwig Wittgenstein

Philosopher of logic and language

All the propositions of logic are generalizations of tautologies and all generalizations of tautologies are generalizations of logic. There are no other logical propositions

  • 1889: Born in Vienna, Austria.
  • 1906-1913: Studies mathematics and philosophy at the University of Vienna.
  • 1913-1914: Moves to Cambridge to study with Bertrand Russell.
  • 1914-1918: Serves as a soldier in the First World War.
  • 1918: Begins work on his major work, "Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus".
  • 1919: Receives his doctorate in philosophy from the University of Vienna.
  • 1921: Publishes "Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus".
  • 1922: Retires from academic life and works as a rural teacher in Austria.
  • 1923: Begins work as an engineer in an engineering factory in Vienna.
  • 1926: Returns to Cambridge to continue his work in philosophy.
  • 1929: Begins work on his new work, "Philosophical Investigations."
  • 1930: Returns to Austria and begins work as a rural teacher again.
  • 1933: Publishes "Philosophische Bemerkungen".
  • 1939: Begins work as a lecturer at Cambridge University.
  • 1944: Publishes "Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus" in its second edition.
  • 1947: Publishes "Philosophical Investigations".
  • 1951: Leaves Cambridge and retires from academic life.
  • 1951-1952: Teaches at Oxford University.
  • 1952: Publishes "Remarks on the Foundations of Mathematics".
  • 1953: Dies in Cambridge, England.

Main thesis of the Tractatus

Language describes reality in a logical way and true propositions are those that correspond to reality.

Main thesis of the Philosophical Investigations

Language and thought are more complex and interconnected than previously thought, and understanding how we use language is essential to understanding philosophy.
